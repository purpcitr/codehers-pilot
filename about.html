Who We Are

The mission of our initiative is to enable girls and other underrepresented groups to envision themselves in computer science, and that starts with ensuring that our students are able to see people like them teaching and organizing our classes.

We have team members headed to Yale, Princeton, and Columbia — and even team members who have yet to begin their high school journeys. Among our numbers are a LEDA scholar, a QuestBridge College Prep Scholar, a U.S. Presidential Scholar, and other student leaders. Scroll down to learn more about our volunteer team!

Alice Ao

Founder, Operations Director

Alice Ao is a first-year at Yale University in New Haven, CT. She’s spent the past few years working to bridge the gender gap in STEM, and she’s been recognized for her work as a U.S. Presidential Scholar, SWENext Global Innovator, and NCWIT Affiliate Award Winner. Her favorite pastimes include reading novels, watching cheesy rom coms, and pestering her younger siblings.

Christine Ao

Founder, Executive Director

Christine is a junior at Peachtree Ridge High School. As the vice president of Mu Alpha Theta and Girls Who Code at her school and a Creative Director for Reinvented Magazine, Christine is passionate about encouraging other girls to pursue STEM. When she’s not learning or teaching coding, Christine can be found upcycling bucket hats out of old jeans, baking banana bread, and painting over her walls.

Jade Li

Jade is a senior at Dublin High School in the Bay Area. Aside from coding, she enjoys spending time with her bunny and rewatching sitcoms. Jade is also a member of the Dublin High Cross Country team and team captain of the Science Olympiad club.
